# Friction Register

**Layer 1 · Lifecycle Map · Template 05**

This is the running log of where the service has friction. It is one of the two things Layer 1 hands forward (the other is the list of decisions), and the Mapping stage is judged on it. Layer 2 weighs opportunity against friction, so a vague register produces vague opportunities. Keep it grounded in evidence, not impression.

Build it as you go. Every interview, every observation, every breaking point in the blueprint, every exception in the process is a possible entry. AI drafts possible entries from the tagged interviews, each showing the words that prompted it; you keep, merge, or cut them. Log first, refine later.

## How to use this register

Each friction point gets an ID (FR-01, FR-02, ...) and a place on the map, so the journey map, blueprint, and process documentation can all point to it.

Keep a friction point separate from a solution. A friction point says what is wrong and how you know. It does not name a fix. If a solution suggests itself while you log, set it aside; Layer 2 is where solutions get proposed and tested against value.

Ground severity and how-often in evidence. "Severe" should mean you can say who is harmed and how much. "Often" should mean you can point to how often, even roughly. Where you only have an impression, say so in the evidence column rather than inflating the rating.

### Friction types

Use these to sort each point. A point can be more than one type; list the main one first.

- **Delay**: work waits, queues, or stalls.
- **Rework**: something has to be redone because it was wrong, incomplete, or lost.
- **Error**: the service produces a wrong result.
- **Handoff gap**: work drops, doubles up, or distorts when it moves between people or systems.
- **Effort**: the person or staff have to work far harder than they should to get a routine result.
- **Experience**: the service is confusing, opaque, or demoralizing, even when it technically works.
- **Risk**: the service exposes the university or the person to harm (compliance, privacy, safety, equity).
- **Capacity**: demand outruns the people or systems there to meet it.

## Severity guide

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| High | Causes wrong results, real harm, real cost, or makes people give up on the service. |
| Moderate | Slows or frustrates the service noticeably, but people work around it. |
| Low | A nuisance; people notice it but it does not change results. |

## Clusters

Once the register has some body, group related friction. Clusters often show that a dozen small complaints share one root, and a root is easier for Layer 2 to act on than a scattered list. AI helps here: it groups related friction across all the interviews at once and flags where several people independently hit the same thing, which is a sign of how often it happens that a single reader would miss. You decide which groupings hold.

## Friction summary

When mapping is nearly done, write a plain-language summary of where this service has the most friction. Three to five sentences. The lead writes this, not the AI. It goes into the validation packet, and the lifecycle owner reacts to it.
